Classic Chicken Parmesan

~Description~

Chicken Parmesan is a beloved Italian-American dish featuring crispy breaded chicken cutlets smothered in marinara sauce and melted cheese, then baked to perfection and often served over pasta for a satisfying meal.

~Ingredients~

    For the breaded chicken:
  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1½ lbs / 680g)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1½ cups (150g) Italian-seasoned breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
    For assembly:
  • 2 cups (500ml) marinara sauce (jarred or homemade)
  • 8 oz (225g) fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced (or 1½ cups shredded)
  • ½ cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
    For serving:
  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti, cooked according to package
  • Extra marinara sauce, warmed

~Steps~

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Lightly grease a baking dish or line a baking sheet with foil.
  2. Pound chicken breasts between plastic wrap to an even ½-inch thickness. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Set up a breading station: Place flour in one shallow bowl, beaten eggs in another, and mix breadcrumbs with ½ cup Parmesan in a third.
  4. Dredge each chicken breast in flour (shake off excess), dip in egg, then coat evenly in breadcrumb mixture. Press gently to adhere.
  5. Heat ¼-inch oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry chicken 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown. Transfer to paper towels to drain.
  6. Spread a thin layer of marinara in the baking dish. Place fried chicken on top. Spoon more marinara over each piece, then top with mozzarella slices and remaining Parmesan.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes until cheese is melted, bubbly, and lightly golden.
  8. Let rest 5 minutes. Serve hot over spaghetti with extra sauce and garnished with fresh herbs. Enjoy!
